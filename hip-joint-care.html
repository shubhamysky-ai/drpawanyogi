Overview

Advanced Hip & Joint Management

Joint pain can affect any joint in the body, but the weight-bearing joints—such as the hips, knees, and ankles—are most prone to wear and tear. Conditions like osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, bursitis, and tendonitis cause chronic inflammation, cartridge loss, stiffness, and severe discomfort during movements.

At Shri Shanti Physiotherapy Center, Dr. Pawan Yogi provides specialized joint wellness protocols. We combine manual adjustments, passive joint distraction techniques, ultrasound therapy, and non-impact strengthening (like hydrotherapy-style exercises or isometric loading) to reduce inflammation, improve joint fluid circulation, and strengthen support muscles to protect the joint faces.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can physiotherapy delay or prevent hip replacement surgery?

Yes. For patients with early-to-moderate hip osteoarthritis, strengthening the surrounding hip stabilizers (gluteal and core muscles) and restoring capsular flexibility can significantly delay or even eliminate the need for joint replacement surgery.

What exercises are best for arthritic joints?

Low-impact, non-weight-bearing exercises are ideal. Swimming, stationary cycling, isometric quadriceps sets, and gentle hamstring stretches build joint support without aggravating joint surface friction.

When should physical therapy start after hip replacement surgery (THR)?

In-hospital physical therapy starts within 24 hours of surgery, focusing on ankle pumps, bed transfers, and early walking with a walker. Outpatient clinic physiotherapy begins around week 3 to rebuild gait balance and muscle strength.

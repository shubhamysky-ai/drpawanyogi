Overview

Rebuilding Athletic Performance

Sports injuries involve damage to the musculoskeletal system, including muscles, bones, tendons, ligaments, and cartilage, sustained during athletic activities. Common injuries include sprains, strains, joint dislocations, and ligament tears (like ACL/meniscus tears).

At Shri Shanti Physiotherapy Center, Dr. Pawan Yogi provides specialized sports rehabilitation programs. Rather than just resting, we focus on dynamic recovery—analyzing movement mechanics, muscle recruitment balance, and sport-specific loads. We use targeted progressive resistive training and therapeutic modalities to restore stability, speed, and agility, preventing future re-injury.

Common Sports Injuries

  • ACL & Meniscus tears (Knee)
  • Ankle Sprains & ligament stress
  • Muscle strains (Hamstring, Groin)
  • Tennis elbow & shoulder rotator cuff issues

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

When can I safely return to my sport after an injury?

Return to play depends entirely on tissue healing and functional testing. Minor sprains take 2 to 4 weeks. Major ligament surgeries like ACL reconstruction require 6 to 9 months of systematic, structured rehabilitation to ensure the joint is stable.

How is sports physiotherapy different from general physiotherapy?

General physiotherapy focuses on restoring pain-free daily activities. Sports physiotherapy demands higher recovery goals, including sport-specific agility training, power development, biomechanical strain correction, and heavy load management.

What should I do immediately after a sports injury?

Follow the modern POLICE protocol: Protect the joint, Optimally Load (avoid total rest unless fractured), Ice (for swelling), Compress (elastic bandage), and Elevate. Then visit us for a clinical diagnostic check.

Overview

Advanced SCI Neurological Rehab

A Spinal Cord Injury (SCI) involves damage to the spinal cord, resulting in a temporary or permanent loss of sensory and motor functions below the site of injury. Depending on whether the injury is cervical, thoracic, or lumbar, it leads to varying degrees of paraplegia (paralysis of legs) or quadriplegia (paralysis of arms and legs).

At Shri Shanti Physiotherapy Center, Dr. Pawan Yogi provides intensive neurological rehabilitation programs. We use specialized motor re-education protocols, passive and active mobilization, neuromuscular electrical stimulation, and coordinate exercises to maximize neural plasticity, prevent joint stiffness, and help patients achieve maximum functional independence.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can patients walk again after a spinal cord injury?

Recovery depends on whether the injury is 'complete' (total nerve cutoff) or 'incomplete' (some nerve fibers intact). Many incomplete SCI patients achieve standing or assisted walking, while complete SCI patients focus heavily on upper-body strength and wheelchair mobility independence.

How does physiotherapy help prevent joint contractures?

Due to lack of movement, paralyzed muscles and tendons shorten over time, locking joints (contractures). Structured daily passive stretching and range-of-motion therapy ensure tissue length is maintained for future recovery potential.

How long should spinal cord injury rehabilitation continue?

Rehabilitation begins immediately post-injury and continues intensively for the first 12 to 24 months, which is the window of maximum neurological healing. Maintenance physical therapy is recommended long-term to prevent secondary complications.

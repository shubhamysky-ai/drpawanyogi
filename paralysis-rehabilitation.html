Overview

Advanced Neurological Rehabilitation

Paralysis is the loss of muscle function in a part of the body, often accompanied by a loss of sensation. It is primarily caused by damage to the nervous system, particularly the brain or spinal cord, resulting from conditions like strokes, trauma, Bell's palsy, or neurological diseases.

As a specialist holding an MPT in Neurology, Dr. Pawan Yogi utilizes clinical neuroscience and physical rehabilitation to help patients reclaim motor control. Our center specializes in the PNF Approach (Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation), which facilitates muscle contractions, improves joint range, and rewires brain-to-muscle coordination pathway connections (Neuroplasticity).

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

When should paralysis rehabilitation begin after a stroke?

As soon as the patient is medically stable. Early intervention within the first 3 to 6 months is crucial, as the brain exhibits high neuroplasticity, maximizing the chances of motor function recovery.

What is the role of the PNF approach in neuro-rehab?

PNF (Proprioceptive Neuromuscular Facilitation) is a method of stretching and exercising that facilitates muscular contractions and motor responses by stimulating the body's natural position sensors (proprioceptors).

How long does paralysis rehabilitation usually take?

Neurological rehabilitation is a gradual process. Significant coordination improvements can occur within 6 to 12 weeks of intensive therapy, though long-term progress continues over several months.
